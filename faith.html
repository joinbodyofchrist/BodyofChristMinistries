Statement of Faith

The core biblical beliefs that ground and guide everything we do.

1. The Holy Scriptures

We believe the Bible — consisting of the 66 books of the Old and New Testaments — to be the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. It is the supreme and final authority in all matters of faith and conduct (2 Timothy 3:16–17; 2 Peter 1:20–21).

2. The Trinity

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — each fully God, equal in nature, distinct in person, and united in purpose (Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14).

3. Jesus Christ

We believe in the full deity and full humanity of Jesus Christ; in His virgin birth, sinless life, and atoning death; in His bodily resurrection, ascension to the Father's right hand, and His coming return in power and glory (John 1:1,14; 1 Corinthians 15:3–4; Acts 1:11).

4. The Holy Spirit

We believe in the person and work of the Holy Spirit who convicts the world of sin, regenerates believers, indwells them, and empowers them for holy living and service (John 16:8–11; Romans 8:9; Acts 1:8).

5. Salvation

We believe salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. Every person is fallen by sin and in need of redemption. Eternal life is the free gift of God to all who repent and trust in Jesus as Lord and Savior (Ephesians 2:8–9; Romans 3:23–24; John 3:16).

6. The Church

We believe the Church is the body of Christ, comprising all who are born again by the Spirit of God. The local church is called to preach the Gospel, celebrate the sacraments of baptism and the Lord's Supper, and make disciples of all nations (Matthew 16:18; 28:19–20; Ephesians 1:22–23).

7. Prayer

We believe prayer is a vital means of grace by which we commune with God, make our requests known, and align ourselves with His will. We are called to pray without ceasing and to intercede for one another (Philippians 4:6; 1 Thessalonians 5:17; James 5:16).

8. The Resurrection and Eternal Life

We believe in the bodily resurrection of all people — the saved to eternal life with God, and the unsaved to eternal separation from Him. Our hope is fixed on the return of Christ and the renewal of all things (John 5:28–29; Revelation 21:3–5).

If you have questions about these beliefs or would like to speak with a minister, please contact us — we'd love to talk with you.
